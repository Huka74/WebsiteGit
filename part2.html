A black-body is an idealized physical object, that is said to absorb all light, regardless of its frequency or incidence angle. Its property of absorbing all incoming light give its name of black-body. However, it is not necessarly actually black, as we will see in this blog, a black-body still emits radiation. The concept of black-body is useful to provide a theoretical framwork, the simplest and most universal emitter. As all light is absorbed, there is no reflection or scaterring, making the emission independent of the object shape or material. The emitted light depends solely on its internal energy distribution, making it a good tool to understand thermal radiation. Before
